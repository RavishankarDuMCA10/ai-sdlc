## Overview

### Background

Our company has an established presence in the car sales business and is now expanding into car rental as a new line of business. While core operational processes (fleet management, customer bookings, etc.) are being built for the rental business, the accounting function requires dedicated capabilities to manage the financial aspects of car rentals accurately and compliantly. Car rental accounting differs from car sales accounting in several ways: revenue is earned over the duration of each rental period, deposits must be held and eventually settled, and recurring or corporate billing patterns introduce complexity not present in one-off vehicle sales.

### Objective

To deliver an accounting module within the car rental system that accurately captures, tracks, and reports all financial activity generated by car rental operations — including rental income, customer deposits, invoices, refunds, and tax obligations — and that integrates seamlessly with the company's existing financial ecosystem.

### Goals

- Maintain a complete and accurate record of all financial transactions related to car rentals (income, expenses, deposits, refunds, adjustments).
- Ensure all financial processes comply with applicable accounting standards and local tax or regulatory requirements.
- Provide flexible invoicing capabilities that support individual, corporate, and recurring billing scenarios.
- Enable integration with existing or future accounting software to avoid double-entry and data silos.
- Deliver actionable financial reports (profit/loss, outstanding balances, tax summaries) that support management decision-making.
- Support multi-currency transactions and multiple tax regimes for future geographic expansion.
- Enforce internal controls and approval workflows to safeguard against financial errors or fraud.
- Retain accounting records in accordance with legal and internal data-retention policies.

## Problem Statement

### User Pain Points and Business Problem

The company currently has no system or process designed specifically for car rental accounting. Key financial challenges expected without a dedicated module include:

- **Revenue recognition complexity:** Rental income spans multiple days or weeks; without proper tracking, income may be recognised incorrectly or at the wrong period.
- **Deposit mismanagement:** Customer deposits (security bonds) must be clearly separated from earned revenue, tracked individually, and refunded or forfeited under specific conditions. Mixing deposits with income creates reconciliation errors and potential legal exposure.
- **Manual invoicing overhead:** Generating invoices for individual customers, corporate accounts, and recurring contracts manually is error-prone and time-consuming.
- **Disconnected financial data:** Without integration between the rental operations system and accounting software, finance staff must manually re-enter transactions, increasing risk of data discrepancies.
- **Inadequate reporting:** Management lacks real-time visibility into rental profitability, outstanding payments, and tax liabilities.
- **Compliance risk:** Failure to adhere to applicable accounting standards (e.g. IFRS 16 for lease accounting) or tax regulations exposes the company to audits, penalties, or reputational damage.
- **Refund and chargeback inconsistency:** Ad-hoc handling of refunds and chargebacks creates unreconciled entries and disputes with customers.

### Key Users

| User Role | Description |
|---|---|
| **Accountant / Finance Officer** | Records financial transactions, manages invoices, processes deposits, and prepares financial reports. |
| **Finance Manager** | Reviews and approves high-value or sensitive financial transactions; accesses management reports. |
| **Operations Staff** | Creates rental bookings; their actions trigger financial events (deposits, charges) in the accounting module. |
| **Corporate Client** | Receives consolidated or recurring invoices for their organisation's rental usage. |
| **Customer (Individual)** | Makes rental payments, pays deposits, and may request refunds. |
| **IT / Integration Administrator** | Configures and maintains integrations with external accounting software. |
| **Auditor / Compliance Officer** | Reviews financial records, audit trails, and data-retention compliance. |

### Why This Is Important Now

The car rental business line is being launched, and financial processes must be in place from day one to ensure revenue is captured accurately, customers are billed correctly, and the company meets its tax and regulatory obligations. Establishing a solid accounting module at launch prevents costly retroactive reconciliation, avoids regulatory non-compliance, and builds customer trust through transparent and accurate billing.

## Functional Requirements

### FR-1: Financial Record Management

#### US-1.1: Record Rental Income
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to automatically create a rental income entry for every completed or ongoing rental booking, **So that** I have an accurate and up-to-date record of all rental revenue without manual data entry.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - A rental income transaction is created when a rental booking is confirmed and updated upon completion.
  - The entry records: booking reference, customer, vehicle, rental period, currency, gross amount, applicable taxes, and net amount.
  - Income entries are linked to the originating booking record.

#### US-1.2: Record Operating Expenses
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to support recording of operating expenses related to car rentals (e.g. vehicle maintenance, insurance, fuel, cleaning), **So that** I can accurately calculate the profitability of the rental business.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Expenses can be created manually or imported; they must be categorised (maintenance, insurance, etc.).
  - Each expense entry records: date, category, amount, currency, vehicle reference (if applicable), and description.
  - Expenses are available for inclusion in profit/loss reports.

#### US-1.3: Track Outstanding Balances
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to maintain a real-time record of outstanding (unpaid) rental charges per customer or corporate account, **So that** I can follow up on overdue payments promptly.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Outstanding balances are displayed per customer and per booking.
  - The system flags balances overdue beyond a configurable number of days.
  - An outstanding payments report can be generated on demand.

---

### FR-2: Compliance with Accounting Standards

#### US-2.1: Comply with Applicable Accounting Standards
- **Statement:** **As a** finance manager, **I want** the system's financial records and reporting to align with applicable accounting standards (e.g. IFRS, local GAAP), **So that** the company meets its statutory and audit obligations.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Revenue recognition follows the accrual basis; rental income is recognised over the rental period, not solely at payment receipt.
  - The system supports audit-ready transaction records with timestamps, user attribution, and immutable history.
  - Configuration options allow the finance team to specify the applicable accounting framework (e.g. IFRS, local GAAP).

#### US-2.2: Support Tax Compliance
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to calculate and record applicable taxes (e.g. VAT, GST, sales tax) on every rental transaction, **So that** tax submissions are accurate and can be prepared without manual recalculation.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Tax rules (rate, type, applicability) are configurable per jurisdiction/region.
  - Tax amounts are displayed separately on invoices and in financial records.
  - A tax liability summary report is available for each reporting period.

### FR-3: Invoice Management

#### US-3.1: Generate Individual Customer Invoices
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to automatically generate an invoice for each individual rental transaction, **So that** customers receive accurate and timely billing with no manual effort.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - An invoice is generated upon rental completion or at configurable billing trigger points.
  - Each invoice includes: invoice number, customer details, rental period, itemised charges, taxes, total amount due, and payment instructions.
  - Invoices can be delivered via email or downloaded as PDF.

#### US-3.2: Generate Corporate Invoices
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to consolidate multiple rental bookings for a corporate client into a single invoice (daily, weekly, or monthly), **So that** corporate clients receive clear consolidated billing aligned with their preferred payment terms.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Corporate clients can be configured with a billing cycle (daily, weekly, monthly) and payment terms (e.g. net 30).
  - A consolidated invoice is generated automatically at the end of each billing cycle.
  - The consolidated invoice lists each individual rental as a line item.

#### US-3.3: Manage Recurring Invoices
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to create and manage recurring invoices for long-term rental agreements, **So that** clients on subscription or long-term contracts are billed automatically without manual intervention each period.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Recurring invoice schedules can be created, paused, or cancelled.
  - The system generates and sends recurring invoices automatically on the configured schedule.
  - Any changes to the recurring amount (e.g. price adjustments) are applied from the next invoice cycle and logged.

---

### FR-4: Customer Deposit Management

#### US-4.1: Record and Track Refundable Deposits
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to record security deposits collected from customers as liabilities (not income), **So that** they are tracked separately and returned to the customer when eligibility for a refund is confirmed.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - A deposit entry is created at booking and flagged as a liability (refundable).
  - The deposit status tracks: collected, held, applied (to charges), and refunded.
  - The system prevents a deposit from being recognised as income until it is legitimately forfeited.

#### US-4.2: Manage Non-Refundable Deposits and Fees
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to record and immediately recognise non-refundable deposits or fees as income at the time of collection, **So that** the financial records correctly reflect earned revenue.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Non-refundable deposits or fees are marked as income at collection.
  - The system maintains a clear audit trail distinguishing refundable versus non-refundable amounts.

#### US-4.3: Process Deposit Refunds
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to facilitate the refund of eligible deposits back to the customer, **So that** refunds are processed accurately and the liability is cleared from the books.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - A refund request can be initiated from the deposit record.
  - Partial refunds are supported (e.g. when deductions for damage apply).
  - The deposit liability is reduced by the refunded amount upon confirmation of refund payment.

### FR-5: Accounting Software Integration

#### US-5.1: Export Financial Data to Accounting Software
- **Statement:** **As a** IT/integration administrator, **I want** the system to export financial transactions (income, expenses, invoices, deposits, refunds) to the company's accounting software (e.g. Xero, QuickBooks, SAP), **So that** there is no need for manual double-entry and the general ledger is always up to date.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - The system supports at least one standard integration method (e.g. API connector, CSV export, direct sync).
  - Financial records are mapped to the corresponding chart-of-accounts entries in the target accounting software.
  - Integration logs are maintained and errors are surfaced for review and resolution.

#### US-5.2: Configure Integration Settings
- **Statement:** **As a** IT/integration administrator, **I want** to configure the integration parameters (authentication, account mappings, sync frequency) from within the system's admin interface, **So that** the integration can be maintained without custom development for each change.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - An admin screen allows configuration of integration endpoints, credentials (stored securely), field mappings, and sync schedules.
  - Changes to configuration take effect without system restart.
  - Configuration changes are logged with user and timestamp.

### FR-6: Financial Reporting

#### US-6.1: Profit and Loss Report
- **Statement:** **As a** finance manager, **I want** to generate a profit and loss (P&L) report for the car rental business for any selected time period, **So that** I can evaluate the financial performance of the rental business line.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - The P&L report displays total rental income, itemised operating expenses, gross profit, and net profit for the selected period.
  - The report can be filtered by time period (day, week, month, custom range) and exported in PDF or spreadsheet format.

#### US-6.2: Outstanding Payments Report
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** to generate a report of all outstanding (unpaid) invoices and their ageing, **So that** I can prioritise collections and manage cash flow effectively.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - The report lists overdue invoices grouped by ageing buckets (e.g. 0–30 days, 31–60 days, 60+ days).
  - Each entry shows: customer name, invoice number, original amount, outstanding balance, and due date.
  - The report can be filtered by customer type (individual or corporate).

#### US-6.3: Tax Report
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** to generate a tax summary report for each reporting period, **So that** I can prepare and submit accurate tax returns.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - The report summarises tax collected by tax type and jurisdiction for the selected period.
  - The report shows taxable revenue, tax rate applied, and total tax collected.
  - The report can be exported in formats compatible with common tax submission tools.

### FR-7: Multi-Currency and Multi-Tax Support

#### US-7.1: Process Transactions in Multiple Currencies
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to support transactions in multiple currencies, with automatic or manually-entered exchange rates, **So that** the company can serve international customers without separate manual currency conversion.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Each transaction can be denominated in any supported currency.
  - Exchange rates are configurable (manually updated or fetched from a rate source); the rate used is recorded against each transaction.
  - All reporting can be presented in a configured base (reporting) currency, with foreign-currency amounts converted at the recorded rate.

#### US-7.2: Apply Multiple Tax Regimes
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** the system to support different tax types and rates for different geographic regions or customer categories, **So that** the correct tax is applied regardless of where or to whom a rental is billed.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Tax rules (type, rate, applicability conditions) are configurable per country/region.
  - The system automatically applies the correct tax rule based on the rental location or customer tax profile.
  - Tax rules can be updated without requiring system downtime.

### FR-8: Refunds, Adjustments, and Chargebacks

#### US-8.1: Process Rental Refunds
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** to process full or partial refunds for completed rentals where the customer is entitled to a reimbursement, **So that** the refund is accurately recorded, the original invoice is adjusted, and the customer receives their money.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - A refund can be initiated from any paid invoice.
  - Full or partial refunds are supported; a reason must be selected or entered.
  - The refund amount is recorded as a credit and the customer's balance updated accordingly.
  - A credit note or refund document is generated and can be sent to the customer.

#### US-8.2: Record Revenue Adjustments
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** to record adjustments to rental charges (e.g. corrections, discounts applied post-invoice), **So that** the financial records remain accurate without deleting original entries.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Adjustments create a new correcting journal entry linked to the original transaction.
  - Each adjustment requires a reason and is attributed to the user who made it.
  - Original entries are never overwritten; only the net position is updated.

#### US-8.3: Manage Chargebacks
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** to record and track chargebacks initiated by customers through their bank or payment provider, **So that** the company can respond to disputes and maintain accurate accounts receivable.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - A chargeback record can be created, linked to the original invoice, and tracked through statuses: received, under review, won, and lost.
  - If a chargeback is lost, the corresponding income is reversed in the accounts.
  - A chargeback summary report is available.

### FR-9: Internal Controls and Approval Workflows

#### US-9.1: Require Approval for High-Value Transactions
- **Statement:** **As a** finance manager, **I want** the system to route high-value or sensitive financial transactions (e.g. refunds or adjustments above a configurable threshold) for approval before they are finalised, **So that** there is appropriate oversight and the risk of errors or fraud is reduced.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Approval thresholds (by transaction type and amount) are configurable by an administrator.
  - Transactions meeting the threshold are placed in a pending state and a notification is sent to the designated approver(s).
  - The approver can approve or reject the transaction, with a mandatory comment on rejection.
  - Only approved transactions are posted to the accounts.

#### US-9.2: Segregation of Duties
- **Statement:** **As a** finance manager, **I want** the system to enforce role-based access controls (preventing the same person from both creating and approving a transaction), **So that** basic segregation-of-duties controls are maintained.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - User roles define create, review, and approve permissions separately.
  - The system prevents the same user from both initiating and approving the same transaction.
  - Access control settings are managed by a designated administrator.

#### US-9.3: Audit Trail
- **Statement:** **As a** auditor, **I want** the system to maintain an immutable audit trail for all financial transactions and configuration changes, **So that** every action is traceable to a user and timestamp for compliance and investigation purposes.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Every create, update, and approve action on a financial record is logged with user ID, timestamp, and a before/after snapshot of key fields.
  - Audit logs cannot be edited or deleted by any user.
  - Audit logs can be filtered by user, date range, and transaction type, and exported in a standard format.

---

### FR-10: Data Retention and Audit Trail

#### US-10.1: Configurable Data Retention Policies
- **Statement:** **As a** compliance officer, **I want** to configure data retention periods for different categories of financial records (e.g. invoices, transaction logs, audit trails), **So that** the company complies with applicable legal and regulatory retention requirements.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Retention periods are configurable per record type (e.g. invoices retained for 7 years, audit logs for 10 years).
  - Records past their retention period are flagged for archiving or deletion according to the configured policy.
  - Any automated deletion or archiving action is logged before execution.

#### US-10.2: Archive and Retrieve Historical Records
- **Statement:** **As a** accountant, **I want** to archive older financial records that are no longer in active use and retrieve them when required (e.g. for an audit), **So that** the active database remains performant while historical data remains accessible.

- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Archived records can be retrieved by authorised users on demand.
  - Archiving does not alter or corrupt the original record.
  - Archived records remain searchable by key fields (date range, customer, invoice number).
